There's lots of great things you can do with styling inputs. Here's a little bit to get you started. But I would definitely recommend playing with this input in a Codepen

  • Select this input: {{selectorType}}
  • Psuedo selectors are going to be your best friend when styling your inputs. For instance, you can grab the field when its focused? disabled? invalid? All with psuedo selectors! Just add the colon and proper psuedo selector after. 
    {{selectorType}}:focus, {{selectorType}}:invalid,
                {{selectorType}}:required
  • How about all selectors except checkboxes? With modern browsers, its no problem! 
    input:not[type="checkbox"]
  • Or you can chain selectors together! 
    {{selectorType}}:focus:invalid // will only apply styles
                to fields that are focused and invalid at the same time.
  • In case you can't tell. I'm pretty passionate about styling forms. Just remember, there's lots you can do with styling. And now with all these great selectors. Your possibilities are so much wider.
